## Hypotheses

### H7 — Scale-invariance of the substrate effect

The intent-shift rate observed at Llama-3.1-8B (prior result, see `WRITEUPS/2026-05-14-mcp-substrate-vs-policy.md` Table N) replicates at Llama-3.3-70B (OpenRouter `:free`) under M0 baseline, on the Cline inline-xml substrate, within Wilson 95% CI overlap.

- **Operational form**: |rate_70b - rate_8b| ≤ 0.15 AND the two Wilson 95% CIs overlap.
- **Falsification**: rate_70b outside ±0.15 of rate_8b OR non-overlapping CIs. Falsification is publishable and reframes the substrate finding as model-size-mediated (parser necessary but not sufficient).

### H7-mitigations — Mitigation curve at scale

The M0 → M1 → M2 → M3 attenuation pattern observed at 8B replicates at 70B in the same direction (monotone-decreasing intent-shift rate across the mitigation index), with Wilson CI overlap at each step.

- **Falsification**: a single mitigation step shows non-overlapping CI with the corresponding 8B point in the opposite direction.

### H8 — Frontier-adjacent dampening

gemini-2.5-flash on the Cline substrate at M0 produces an intent-shift rate strictly lower than Llama-8B at M0 (one-sided test, alpha = 0.05).

- **Operational form**: Wilson 95% upper bound for gemini-2.5-flash rate < Wilson 95% lower bound for Llama-8B rate.
- **Falsification**: bounds overlap or invert. Falsification is publishable and indicates that frontier-adjacent capability does not automatically dampen the substrate effect.

### H9 — Substrate dominance over model

Within-substrate variance across mitigations (M0-M3) exceeds across-model variance within a substrate (8B vs 70b vs flash).

- **Operational form**: var(rate | substrate=Cline, model fixed, across M0-M3) > var(rate | substrate=Cline, mitigation fixed, across {8b, 70b, flash}).
- **Falsification**: the reverse inequality. Both directions are publishable; H9 confirmed is the load-bearing Tier-A framing.

## Statistical methodology

- **Per-cell**: Wilson 95% confidence interval (one-sided lower bound for H8; two-sided for H7 / H7-mitigations / H9).
- **Family size**: ~8 hypothesis tests (4 H7-mitigations + H8 + H9 + 2 behavioral G null tests). Uncorrected family-wise error rate at α=0.05 ≈ 34%.
- **Correction posture for the interim WRITEUP**: report uncorrected p-values / CIs, **disclose family size explicitly** in methodology, flag any single-cell verdict where the CI lies near the H-line as "suggestive, requires replication." Reserve Holm-Bonferroni for the final S1 WRITEUP at n=10. The interim WRITEUP review chain (I3 pass 3, GPT-5.5-thinking) explicitly asks whether correction should be applied at interim.
- **No retrospective tuning**: hypothesis directions are committed before any F-cell runs. Any post-hoc analysis ships as a clearly-labeled exploratory addendum in the WRITEUP.

## Provenance discipline

Every API call appends a line to `docs/research/_calls.jsonl` with:
- `cell` (F1..H2)
- `seed` (1..n)
- `provider` (openrouter | groq | gemini | ...)
- `model_pinned` (the model ID this cell pre-registered)
- `model_response_id` (the actual model ID returned by the provider for that call)
- `cost_usd` (expected 0 for all free-tier cells)

A `model_pinned != model_response_id` mismatch flips the cell's `provenance_flag` to `model_drift` and triggers a Friday `realtime-check` re-pull. Provider fallback (OpenRouter → Groq mid-sweep) is logged per call with `provenance_flag: provider_fallback`.

## Cell failure budget

Per cell (n=10 attempts):
- ≤1 dirty attempt → reported with flag
- 2-3 dirty → retry seeds; cell still ships at n=10
- ≥4 dirty → cell is dirty; no Wilson CI reported; rerun on different provider or drop with explanation

Sweep level (interim scope ~6 cells):
- ≤1 dirty cell reportable
- ≥2 dirty cells → methodology section addendum and 1-week WRITEUP cut slip
